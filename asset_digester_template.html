✍ Prompt Crafting Guide

▼

The Problem

AI image generators default to a "generic template" when prompts are vague. If you describe 12 characters using only personality traits ("honest expression," "intense gaze," "analytical look"), you'll get 12 near-identical faces. The AI interprets all of those as the same visual: a medium-build man with brown hair and a beard.

The Fix — Concrete Visual Anchors

Every prompt needs at least 5 of these physical differentiators:

  • Facing direction — "facing 3/4 left", "facing right", "straight ahead"
  • Hair — color, length, style (bald, curly, straight, wild, tied back)
  • Facial hair — none, stubble, goatee, full beard, specific color
  • Skin tone — fair, olive, tan, dark brown, pale, weathered
  • Body type — stocky, thin, broad-shouldered, small, tall
  • Clothing color — a SPECIFIC color per character, not just "robes"
  • Held item / prop — key, scroll, dagger, bread basket, staff
  • Unique feature — scar, headband, crown, torn clothing, eye patch
  • Age — teens, 20s, 50s, elderly
  • Expression + pose cue — "one eyebrow raised", "chin raised defiantly"

Example — Bad vs Good Prompts (Characters)

✗ Bad — Vague
Generate a 2D pixel art portrait of "Bartholomew the Apostle". He should have an honest expression with sharp, discerning eyes. Style: 16-bit retro character art. Background: solid flat magenta (#FF00FF).
✓ Good — Visually Specific
Generate a 2D stylized RPG pixel art bust portrait of "Bartholomew the Apostle" facing straight ahead. He has long straight black hair parted in the middle, is clean-shaven with an angular jawline. Dark olive skin. He wears an orange-amber robe with his arms crossed over his chest. Expression: Honest and forthright, sharp discerning eyes. Style: 16-bit retro character art. Background: solid flat magenta (#FF00FF).

The bad prompt produces the AI's default "brown-haired bearded man." The good prompt specifies hair (long, straight, black, parted), facial hair (none), skin (dark olive), clothing (orange-amber), pose (arms crossed), and facing (straight ahead) — each anchor pushes the output away from the generic default.

Example — Bad vs Good Prompts (Creatures / Sprites)

✗ Bad — Generic
Generate a 2D pixel art sprite of a "Red Herring". It is a small fish colored in red. It should look deceptive. Background: solid flat magenta (#FF00FF).
✓ Good — Species-Accurate + Gimmick
Generate a 2D 16-bit retro RPG pixel art sprite of a "Red Herring" facing left with its body angled slightly as if sneaking away. It is an Atlantic Herring — a small, sleek, streamlined fish with a forked tail, a single dorsal fin, and large silvery scales. Colors: deep suspicious crimson-red body with dark charcoal-grey shading along its back. Its eye is narrow, shifty, and side-glancing. A faint dark smoky shadow trails behind it. A tiny question mark hovers above its head. Background: solid flat magenta (#FF00FF).

The bad prompt gives you "a red fish." The good prompt specifies species anatomy (herring body, forked tail, dorsal fin), color distribution (crimson body, charcoal back), facing direction (left, sneaking), expression detail (narrow, shifty eye), and a unique visual gimmick (smoke trail, question mark).

Key Rules

  • No two assets should share the same hair color + clothing color + facing direction.
  • Every character gets a unique held item — this alone differentiates silhouettes at thumbnail size.
  • Specify the background color in every prompt if using chroma key (e.g., "solid flat magenta #FF00FF").
  • Avoid colors that match your chroma key in the subject (e.g., don't say "magenta fins" with a magenta background).
  • For creatures: name the real-world species and its defining anatomy. "Mackerel with wavy vertical stripes" beats "a fish."
  • For groups: specify formation, individual heights/builds, and who stands where. AI struggles with "four men" but handles "diamond formation: front man short and crouching, left man tall with red headwrap."
